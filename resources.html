FAQs

Simply put, zero waste just means producing as little waste as humanly possible. It doesn't necessarily mean produce no waste at all, but rather eliminate the need to throw away products that could be reused, recycled, and composted. This would also prevent materials from going to either a landfill or high-temperature destruction.

At OWO, we believe that achieving zero waste is possible. Having said that, it is our consumer responsibility as residents to reuse items and purchase items that can be recycled or composted.

Here are some useful zero waste tips:

  • Save used, empty food jars or containers to store small miscellaneuous household items as well as other food products, instead of buying one-time use containers.
  • When shopping, bring reusable bags as opposed to using plastic ones.
  • Eliminate one-time use serving ware. Instead, use your own durable utensils that can easily be washed, even if you have disposable ones.
  • Choose and purchase items that are made with recycled content.

Other than recycling, there are others ways to keep the city of Orlando clean again. These include the following:

  • Backyard composting, which city residents can order a free composting bin and start composting food scraps and yard waste in the comfort of their homes.
  • Disposing of hazardous waste by visiting a local hazardous waste drop-off site. Items that have labels containing Flammable, Poison, Warning, Corrosive, Explosive, Danger, and Pesticide are considered hazardous waste.

Luckily, OWO has a blog site that allows you to keep up with other residents in the Orlando community as well as learn about alternative ways to reduce waste. Also, you can check our news & media section on our home page to stay informed on the environmental-related news in Orlando!
